ABSTRACT

Scene graphs should respect how relations transform.

3D Scene Graph Generation (3DSGG) represents scenes as object–relation–object graphs for embodied spatial understanding. In yaw-varying observation frames, directional predicates such as left, right, front, and behind should transform with the frame, whereas approximately yaw-invariant predicates such as standing on and attached to should remain stable. We introduce Transformation-Aware Decoupling (TAD), which separates invariant and direction-sensitive relation reasoning while retaining unified multi-label predicate prediction.

MOTIVATION

One scene, different yaw viewpoints,
different transformation rules.

A shared relation space forces the model to keep some predicates stable while changing others. TAD separates these incompatible requirements.

METHOD

Transformation-Aware Decoupling

TAD retains a unified 26-class predicate head while separating relation reasoning according to predicate transformation behavior.

  1. 01

    Stable object context

    A viewpoint-stable object encoder provides a reliable object-level foundation for relation reasoning.

  2. 02

    Two relation branches

    An invariant branch uses yaw-stable cues, while a directional branch preserves signed offsets and angular cues.

  3. 03

    Unified prediction

    Transformation-specific descriptors and auxiliary supervision encourage complementary factors before unified multi-label predicate prediction.

COUNTERFACTUAL CONTROLS

Why representation-level decoupling?

01

Rotation augmentation

Reduces cross-view variance, but requires four yaw-rotated training copies and remains below TAD.

02

Geometry-rule hybrid

Post-hoc correction can improve rotated-view scores, but hurts canonical-view performance and cannot replace learned relation representations.

03

TAD

Decouples cues, parameters, and objectives so invariant and directional predicates can follow their respective transformation rules.
